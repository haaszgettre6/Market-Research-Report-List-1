<p><p>Allergic rhinitis immunotherapies primarily include subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT). SCIT involves regular injections of allergens to gradually desensitize the immune system, typically requiring a longer treatment duration and clinic visits. In contrast, SLIT involves administering allergen extracts under the tongue, offering a more convenient and less invasive option that can be self-administered at home. Both therapies aim to reduce allergy symptoms and improve quality of life by modifying the body's immune response to specific allergens.</p></p>
