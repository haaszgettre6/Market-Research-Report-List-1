<p><p>Contactless film thickness measurement devices utilize various technologies to gauge material thickness without physical contact. Eddy current methods measure conductive materials by detecting changes in electromagnetic fields. Magnetic induction assesses ferromagnetic surfaces by analyzing induced voltage changes. Optical techniques employ light reflections and interferences to determine thickness, suitable for transparent coatings. Ultrasonic methods utilize sound waves to gauge thickness by measuring reflections, effective for a wide range of material types. Each technology offers unique advantages for specific industrial applications.</p></p>
