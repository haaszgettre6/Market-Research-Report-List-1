<p><ul><li>CMOS</li><li>CCD</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The low-light imaging market comprises two primary sensor types: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device). CMOS sensors are favored for their lower power consumption, faster processing speeds, and integration capabilities, making them ideal for compact devices like smartphones. On the other hand, CCD sensors excel in image quality and sensitivity, making them popular in professional applications such as astronomy and surveillance. Both technologies address distinct needs within low-light environments, driving innovation in imaging solutions.</p></p>
