<p><p>Thermosetting resins are a type of polymer that undergoes a chemical change when cured, resulting in a rigid and inflexible structure. Unlike thermoplastics, which can be remolded upon heating, thermosetting resins maintain their shape and strength even under high temperatures. These materials are commonly used in various industries, including automotive, aerospace, electronics, and construction, for their superior mechanical properties and resistance to heat and chemicals.</p><p>The thermosetting resins market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.4% during the forecast period.
