<p><ul><li>Stereolithography (SLA)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Digital Light Processing (DLP)</li><li>Fused Depositing Modelling (FDM)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The 3D printing in the jewellery market includes several key technologies. Stereolithography (SLA) utilizes UV light to cure liquid resin into solid forms, allowing for intricate designs. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) employs a laser to fuse powdered material, ideal for creating durable pieces. Digital Light Processing (DLP) also uses light to solidify resin but is faster due to its use of projectors. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes filament layer by layer, suitable for prototypes. Other methods may comprise various emerging technologies enhancing design and production.</p></p>
