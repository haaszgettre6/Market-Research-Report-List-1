<p><p>The vaccine market for Porcine Reproductive and Respiratory Syndrome (PRRS) is primarily divided into two types: live vaccines and inactivated vaccines. Live vaccines contain attenuated strains of the virus, providing strong immunity and often requiring fewer doses. In contrast, inactivated vaccines use killed virus particles, offering a safer option with a lower risk of causing disease, though they may require multiple doses for effective immunity. Both types are essential for managing PRRS outbreaks in swine populations, ensuring animal health and farm productivity.</p></p>
