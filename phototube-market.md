<p><ul><li>Gas</li><li>Vacuum</li><li>Electron Multiplier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The phototube market comprises three main types: gas, vacuum, and electron multiplier. Gas phototubes utilize ionization in gases to detect light, often used in low-light applications. Vacuum phototubes operate in a vacuum environment, providing high sensitivity and fast response times for precise measurements. Electron multipliers amplify weak light signals, making them ideal for applications requiring extreme sensitivity, such as in scientific research and high-energy physics. Together, these types cater to diverse industries, including medical, industrial, and scientific sectors.</p></p>
